Get the most from your Google Calendar with these conventions…

**1. Booking Tags**

   To manage booking/payment info, add tags to the end of your event names (#tobook #booked #topay #paid #walkin) to indicate their status.

   Otherwise guests may become unsure if bookings/tickets have been organised.

**2. Filterable Calendars**

   To help your family filter their calendar events more easily, create two shared calendars. ‘Family (Main)’ for events that everyone should see on their month view, and ‘Family (Detail)’ for events that are more relevant to see on their week or day views. This method gives your family the option to “hide” the detail calendar if they just want the big picture.

   If you don’t use 2 family calendars, then events will overcrowd the month view with no means for your family to filter the visual noise, 

**3. Highlight Attendees**

   To indicate which family members should attend which events, ask family members to start event names with the first name initials of who should attend each event, or leave off the initials if the whole family unit should attend together.

   Otherwise family members may be unsure which events are for individuals or for family.

**4. Event Readability**

   To improve your event readability, start the event name with its activity type (e.g. LUNCH MASSAGE MOVIE COFFEE).

   Otherwise, any long event names may be clipped when displayed on small screens and you’ll become unsure what the event is actually about.

**5. Use Task Events**

   To see the full availability of your time, include events in your calendar for tasks you need to do. Starting those event names with ‘-’ or ‘Task:’ to indicate the event is actually a task to complete.

   Otherwise, you won’t see the true time needed to get through your task list for the week.
